Meet Mindfull

Many people struggle with mental health issues, but they lack accessible tools to manage their well-being. Many of the mental wellness apps on the market hide their content behind a paywall. Mindfull addresses these problems by offering a free app that allows users to access digital journaling tools, a meditation player and habit/mood trackers.

Empowering users to be in control of their own mental wellness plan by offering the tools needed and the ability to customizie their experience within the app. Mindfull aims to improve users mental health, reduce stress and enhance overall well-being.

Who is Mindfull for?

Through a series of one on one interviews and a distributed survey a persona began to emerge from the needs, pain points, and varying perspective of our interviewees. We sought to explore the pain points and frustrations individuals face with their current mindfulness practices, if any, and identify barriers preventing others from engaging in mindfulness altogether.

Additionally, we aimed to uncover user expectations for a mental wellness app and gain insights into their preferences for potential features and experiences. This comprehensive understanding would guide us in creating a solution tailored to our users' goals and challenges.

User Interviews

We used an affinity map to organize and analyze data from user interviews, identifying key themes of user pains and gains. This process helped refine our user personas and informed the design of an app that meets user needs.

User Persona

This  is mindful Mark, a midlevel professional somewhere between the ages of 25-35. When he’s not busy with work, he enjoys playing video games and listening to music. He is content with his life, but ultimately has goals of improving his mental health, time management, and productivity levels. He hates to admit this, but he struggles with negative thoughts which impact his ability to focus.

MoSCoW Method

Based on our affinity mapping exercise, we identified recurring user needs and preferences. This informed our selection of key features to be incorporated into the app.

User Journey

Utilizing the data gathered from our persona, the team worked to create a user journey, which would provide an idea of how individuals would interact with app from initial discovery through repeated, long-term engagement.

The user journey was informed largely by the reported pain points and goals of our iterated persona. In the Daily Use and Long Term Engagement sections of the journey, we made a point to focus on solutions to some of the mentioned pain points that users faced

User Flow

Our team individually created user flow after completing user interviews, building our user persona, storyboarding a user journey, and identifying features with the MoSCoW Method. After each team member built a user flow we collaborated together to build a single user flow that our wireframes would be structured around.

Initial Site Map

As a team we built our initial site map to begin building wireframes in figma. We focused on the key features, the elements that needed to be front and center in the design and how to structure the app.

Iterated Site Map

After wireframing we went back and iterated on our site map using the experience to craft a far more detailed guide. We found that during the wireframing process any gaps or flows that could be confusing or did not meet usability heuristic guidelines and we iterated our site map.

Building a Design Guide

Collaboratively our team pulled in inspiration and began to build a style guide. I set up variables within my document to begin to build a design system in my figma workspace so I could work effiecently and quickly.

Dashboard

The dashboard features widgets for users to quickly access certain aspects of the app and to allow them to customize their own wellness plan to suit their needs.

Journal

The journal page features a section for users to save or favorite their favorite entries so they can pull them up later to check. In user interviews we heard a need assitance on how to get started with journaling, so we included 3 prompts on the journal page to select and try.

Meditation

The meditation page features tags in order to filter down the available tracks to play, a daily challenge that would be new meditation tracks for users to try, and categories of tracks to browse through.

Final Thoughts

This was an exciting project to work on and was a chance to showcase all the skills and work of the bootcamp into one final project. I also found the project to be challenging in designing the variety of features to include in Mindfull.

Designing an app that addresses the problems that I feel many people can relate to. Reading medical journals to understand more about exisiting user issues with mental wellness apps and the effect of positive affect journaling when done routinely.
