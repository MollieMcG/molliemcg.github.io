PAWS of Austin's commitment to saving animals is inspiring and we want their website to showcase their level of commitment.

Modernizing an outdated website

Since 2002, PAWS of Austin has served the community by fostering dogs from across Texas. Their outreach extends beyond local borders, encompassing emergency rescue services and collaborative efforts with animal rescue organizations in other states. Recognizing PAWS of Austin's unwavering commitment to service, our team sought to rebuild their website to facilitate greater community engagement with the organization.

The State of the Information Architecture

We identified the existing website as overly complex, characterized by excessive dropdown menus and redundant content within individual pages. This presented an opportunity to streamline the user experience through a comprehensive website redesign.

This process encompassed card sorting exercises, thorough user research, and meticulous site mapping.

Features That Encourage Users

Collaboratively, our team developed a comprehensive style guide to enhance the visual identity of PAWS of Austin, aiming for a more refined and consistent brand aesthetic. While respecting the existing website's color palette, we adjusted the color scheme to mitigate perceived harshness. Recognizing the significance of the organization's location, we incorporated subtle Western and Texan elements into the style guide, subtly reflecting the organization's connection to its local context.

The Redesign

After

Home Page

Building upon a robust foundation of user research and competitive analysis, I initiated the design process by creating a low-fidelity prototype of the application. This initial phase prioritized the establishment of the application's overall structure and functionality.

After

Adoption Page

Utilizing the low-fidelity prototype, I conducted usability testing sessions with a subset of participants from our previous interview pool. Our team collaboratively defined a set of tasks for participants to complete, enabling us to observe their interactions and gather valuable insights through subsequent follow-up questions.

After

Donation Page

Informed by the insights gleaned from usability testing, we embarked on a collaborative design iteration phase. Through collective brainstorming sessions, we addressed the challenges encountered during user testing. Subsequently, each team member independently refined and completed their respective high-fidelity prototypes.

Building a website that inspires others to do good.

Final Thoughts

I found this redesign project both engaging and invigorating. The opportunity to assist an organization in optimizing their website, creating a solution that truly supported their needs rather than hindered them, was particularly exciting. Compared to previous projects, this one placed a greater emphasis on in-depth content analysis, meticulous information architecture planning, and the development of a streamlined user flow. While the project presented several constraints, I discovered that these limitations ultimately sharpened the project's focus, ensuring that efforts were concentrated on the most critical aspects.

  • Focus on the organization's needs and values
  • Identifying the project's scope and approach
  • Our team's approach and outcomes
