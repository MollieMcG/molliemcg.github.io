PAWS of Austin's commitment to saving animals is inspiring and we want their website to showcase their level of commitment.

Bringing an outdated site into the modern era

PAWS of Austin has served their community since 2002 by fostering dogs from all over Texas, their outreach involves emergency rescue services and traveling to other states to aid with animal rescue services.

Our team saw the commitment to service that PAWS of Austin showcased and so we wanted to rebuild their website to better help others become active participants with the organization.

    The redesign focused on...

  • ...a responsive web and mobile design.
  • ...a streamlined adoption process.
  • ...building a donation feature that inspires.
  • ...a new brand identity.

The State of the Information Architecture

We found a site bloated and full of drop down menus, redundancies within the pages themselves and saw an opportunity to trim down the site.

This process involved card sorting, user research, and site mapping.

Features That Encourage Users

First time users of the app upon sign up have the option to complete a little survey that asks them about their level of experience, instruments they’re interested in and their goals. The purpose is to better suggest content and to encourage them via scheduled notifications.

Each course thumbnail includes the amount of videos and the expected time to complete the course. In interviews user’s expressed wanting to see ratings from other students

A repeat comment during interviews was a desire for instructors with experience and proof of skill. With that in mind we wanted a little bio to highlight the instructor of the course.

After

Using the low fidelity prototype I reached out to some of our previous interviewees about doing some usability testing. Our team creaded a list of tasks that we wanted to view being performed, so that we could ask follow up questions about the experience.

With the usability testing information we began our design iterations. This was a joint task as we brainstormed solutions to problems that came up during the tasks performed by users. Individually we then went on to fix and complete our high fidelity prototypes.
